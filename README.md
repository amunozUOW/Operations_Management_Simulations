# Operations Management Simulations

A portfolio of interactive HTML/JavaScript simulations designed for operations management education. These simulations were developed using a GenAI-enabled vibe coding workflow as part of a postgraduate operations management course redesign at the University of Wollongong.

This repository contains supplementary materials accompanying the paper:

> Munoz, A., & Rook, L. (2026). Leveraging Generative AI Through Vibe Coding: A Case of Simulation-Based Curriculum Redesign in Management Education. *Education Sciences*.

## Simulations

Each simulation is a self-contained HTML file that can be opened in any modern web browser or embedded into a Learning Management System (LMS). They are accompanied by teaching notes and lesson plans (provided as `.docx` files) and, where applicable, calculation spreadsheets (`.xlsx` files).

| # | Simulation | Operations Management Topic | Objective |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Number Guessing Game** | Introductory | Introduce students to the importance of decision-making in operations and the analytical and critical thinking required to manage operations. |
| 2 | **The (Un)Productive Food Truck** | Operations performance, productivity | Evaluate performance (single and multi-factor productivity) and understand the role of decisions in driving operations performance. |
| 3 | **Little's Coffee Shop** | Process design, Little's Law | Link capacity, productivity, and Little's Law in operations. |
| 4 | **Supermarket Checkout** | Capacity management | Utilize forecasting to make capacity decisions. |
| 5 | **Vending Machine** | Inventory management | Understand how multiple decisions affect inventory and service levels. |
| 6 | **Mangoes** | Lean operations | Demonstrate use of Economic Order Quantity and how it contrasts with lean principles. |
| 7 | **Supply Chain** | Supply chain management | Manage inventory across multiple locations in a supply chain to ensure synchronized movement of stock and information. |
| 8 | **Red and Blue Hammers** | Quality management | Understand and balance inventory and staffing decisions to ensure customers are served to expectations. |
| 9 | **Garden Project** | Project management | Understand how dynamic allocation of resources affects budget, schedule, and work completion rates. |

## Repository Structure

```
01 Number Guessing Game/
02 The (Un)Productive Food Truck/
03 Littles Coffee Shop/
04 Supermarket Checkout/
05 Vending Machine/
06 Mangoes/
07 Supply Chain/
08 Red and Blue Hammers/
09 Garden Project/
```

Each folder contains:
- An **HTML simulation file** (`.html`) — the interactive simulation
- A **lesson plan or teaching notes** (`.docx`) — instructor guide with background, learning objectives, step-by-step instructions, and discussion questions
- A **calculations spreadsheet** (`.xlsx`, where applicable) — supporting calculations for the underlying operations management models

## Usage

1. **Standalone**: Open any `.html` file directly in a web browser.
2. **LMS Integration**: Upload the `.html` files to your LMS (e.g., Moodle, Canvas, Blackboard) and embed them in course activities.

No server, installation, or programming knowledge is required.
